TOUR INFO

Big Medium is proud to present the 12th East Austin Studio Tour! EAST is a free, self-guided tour that champions the abundance of talent within Austin's large and wildly diverse creative community. The public is invited to discover new artistic talent, see working studios, learn about artists' tools, techniques, and inspirations, and support the arts community by building their collections and supporting local businesses.

COLLECTIVE COURTESY
Please keep in mind that many EAST studios are in residential areas. Our neighbors have been very gracious and welcoming to tour visitors over the years, and it's important to respect and reciprocate that hospitality. Please don't be too loud or cause disturbances; avoid leaving any trash behind and drive & park your cars thoughtfully.

ACCESSIBILITY
Please refer to our online catalog. Under each EAST participant listing in the catalog is a helpful Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) graphic. Each rectangle within the graphic corresponds to a different accessibility feature that is or is not available at the participant's location.

BIG MEDIUM

Big Medium is a non-profit 501(c)(3) arts organization based in Austin, Texas, whose mission is to support the visual arts and artists in Texas. Big Medium proudly produces the East Austin Studio Tour, West Austin Studio Tour and the Texas Biennial, as well as exhibiting innovative contemporary gallery programming in the Big Medium Galleries, providing affordable studio space for artists, and seeking opportunities with various arts organizations and artists to initiate and support unique and creative projects.

For over 10 years, and in more ways than one, Big Medium off of Bolm Road was a home: made by artists and sustained by artists, loose-reigned, built from scratch, and it continues to transform and flourish with a constant flux of fresh minds and ideas. Last spring, Big Medium expanded to the newly renovated Canopy complex at 916 Springdale Road, enabling us to offer a broader variety of studio, exhibition, and event spaces to Austin's ever-growing creative community. The new space and programming is reflective of Big Medium's ambition to see the arts continue to grow and thrive in Austin.
